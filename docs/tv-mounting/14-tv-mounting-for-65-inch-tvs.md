# TV Mounting for 65 Inch TVs

65 inch TVs are the fastest growing segment in UK home entertainment weighing 22-28kg. This guide covers the specific requirements for mounting these heavier TVs including weight distribution, VESA patterns, and installation considerations.

---

## Understanding 65 Inch TV Mounting Requirements

65-inch TVs weigh 22-28kg with larger OLED models at the heavier end. VESA patterns are typically 400x400mm for OLED models and 300x300mm for some LED LCD models. Samsung QLED 65-inch uses 400x400mm. LG OLED65 uses 300x300mm. Sony Bravia 65 uses 400x400mm. The increased size means the wall plate must have sufficient horizontal span. Most 65-inch TV mounts have wall plates 400-600mm wide covering 2-3 brick courses or 1-2 stud bays. Weight ratings must be 2x TV weight minimum. A 65-inch OLED at 25kg needs a mount rated 50kg. Look for heavy-duty mounts with larger wall plates and additional fixing points. Popular 65-inch UK models: LG OLED65C4 (24.5kg, 300x300mm), Samsung QE65QN90D (26.8kg, 400x400mm), Sony XR-65A95L (25.5kg, 400x400mm). For full motion on 65-inch use a heavy-duty dual-arm design rated 45kg+.

---

## Installation Procedure for 65 Inch TVs

Installation requires two people due to size and weight. Step 1: Decide on TV position -- 65-inch TVs need minimum 2.5 metres viewing distance for 4K. Step 2: Wall type assessment is critical. 65-inch TVs require solid brick or concrete walls or timber stud with verified integrity. Never mount a 65-inch TV on plasterboard alone. Step 3: Use heavy-duty fixings -- M10 resin anchors for brick (8mm SDS bit, 80mm depth), M8 coach screws into timber studs. Minimum 6 fixing points on the wall plate. Step 4: Attach VESA brackets using M8x16mm screws (larger than M6 used for smaller TVs). Step 5: Two people lift the TV onto the wall plate using TV lift assist handles. Step 6: Secure locking mechanism. Step 7: Cable management -- 65-inch TVs need right-angle HDMI adapters if using a low-profile mount. Power cable routing: ensure the kettle lead reaches the socket. ## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>Can one person install a 65 inch TV?</dt>
<dd>No, always use two people. 65-inch TVs weigh 22-28kg and are too large and awkward for one person to safely lift and attach to a wall bracket. Attempting solo risks damaging the TV.</dd>

<dt>What VESA pattern does a 65 inch TV use?</dt>
<dd>Most 65-inch OLED TVs use 300x300mm (LG) or 400x400mm (Sony, Panasonic). Samsung QLED 65-inch uses 400x400mm. Check your specific model specifications before buying a mount.</dd>

<dt>Can I mount a 65 inch TV on a stud wall?</dt>
<dd>Yes but only on timber studs with M8 coach screws into the stud centre. Use a spreader bar across at least two studs. Metal studs are not recommended for 65-inch TVs due to insufficient weight capacity.</dd>

<dt>What is the best mount type for a 65 inch TV?</dt>
<dd>Fixed or tilting mounts are the most secure for 65-inch TVs. Full motion mounts need careful selection -- choose a heavy-duty dual-arm model rated at least 2x the TV weight at full extension.</dd>

<dt>What wall fixings do I need for a 65 inch TV on brick?</dt>
<dd>Use M10 resin anchors or expanding shield anchors for brick walls. Minimum 80mm depth into solid brick. Fischer UX 10x50mm wall plugs with 8mm coach screws work but resin anchors provide extra safety margin.</dd>
