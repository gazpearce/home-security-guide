# Smart Plugs and Power Management Guide

Guide to smart plugs and power management for UK homes covering socket types, energy monitoring, automation schedules, remote control, and integration with smart home platforms.

---

## Smart Plug Types and UK Compatibility

Smart plugs are the easiest smart home entry - plug into a UK socket, control via app. Must comply with BS 1363. Popular UK brands: TP-Link Kasa KP105 (£10-£15, WiFi, energy monitoring), Amazon Smart Plug (£20-£25, Alexa only), Hive Active Plug (£25-£30, Zigbee, energy monitoring), Meross MSS110 (£8-£12, WiFi, energy monitoring), IKEA TRADFRI (£12-£15, Zigbee, needs hub). Rated 13A (3120W max) - sufficient for lamps, TVs, small appliances. NOT for heaters or kettles.

---

## Smart Plug Automation Ideas

Energy-saving: Schedule lamps off during daylight. Turn off entertainment when you leave (geofencing). Monitor consumption via app (TP-Link shows real-time watts, kWh, cost at your rate, typically 24-30p/kWh in 2026). Security: Random on/off patterns when away. Turn on radio when motion detected. Outdoor lighting via outdoor-rated smart plug. Convenience: Voice control hard-to-reach lamps. Morning routine: lamp on, kettle on, radio on. Never use with high-power heating appliances unless specifically rated.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>Can smart plugs reduce my electricity bill?</dt>
<dd>Yes - energy monitoring identifies vampire loads. Automating schedules saves £20-£50/year on a typical UK bill.</dd>

<dt>Are smart plugs safe?</dt>
<dd>Yes with BS 1363 certification. They include overload protection. Never use with high-power heating appliances.</dd>

<dt>Can I use smart plugs outdoors?</dt>
<dd>Standard plugs are indoor only (IP20). Buy dedicated outdoor smart plugs with IP44+ rating (£15-£25). For more information see our <a href="../smart-home/03-smart-thermostat-installation-guide-uk.md">Smart Thermostat Installation Guide UK</a>.</dd>
