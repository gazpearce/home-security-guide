# Smart Home Energy Monitoring Guide

Guide to smart home energy monitoring for UK homes covering smart meters, energy monitoring plugs, solar PV integration, EV charging optimisation, and energy-saving automation strategies.

---

## Energy Monitoring Devices for UK Homes

Three levels: smart meter with IHD (free from supplier, SMETS2, real-time usage in £ and kWh, half-hourly data). Energy monitoring plugs (TP-Link Kasa £10-£15, Meross £8-£12) measure individual device consumption. Whole-home monitors (Hildebrand Glow £100-£150, Loop £50-£80) connect via HAN port or CT clamps. Octopus Agile tariff offers half-hourly variable rates (cheap 5p/kWh during windy/solar, expensive 50p/kWh peak). Smart home automation enables shifting high-consumption tasks to cheap periods.

---

## Energy-Saving Automation Strategies

Strategy 1: Vampire elimination - smart plugs on entertainment/computers, turn off at 11pm, on at 6pm. UK standby costs £30-£80/year. Strategy 2: Smart heating - combine thermostat with TRVs, bedrooms 16C, office 18C work hours, living room 21C evening. Tado open window detection. Strategy 3: Solar PV - automate high-consumption devices to run 10am-3pm (peak generation). Set EV charger to use solar surplus. Strategy 4: EV charging - set to start 11.30pm (Octopus Go 7.5p/kWh vs 24p standard). 40kWh charge costs £3 vs £9.60 - saves £800+/year. Strategy 5: Peak avoidance - avoid 4-7pm peak. Typical savings: £200-£500/year for a 3-bed home.

<dd>Yes - £200-£500/year for a 3-bed UK home: heating £100-300, vampires £30-80, smart tariffs £50-200, solar £50-150.</dd>

<dt>Which UK tariff works best?</dt>
