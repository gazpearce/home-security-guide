# Smart Lighting Installation Guide

Guide to installing smart lighting in UK homes covering smart bulbs, smart switches, hub-based systems, room-by-room setup, and automation configuration for energy savings and security.

---

## Smart Lighting Options for UK Homes

Smart lighting comes in two forms: smart bulbs (simplest) and smart switches (requires wiring). UK bulbs use B22 (bayonet) or E27 (screw) fittings. Popular brands: Philips Hue (£15-£60 per bulb, Zigbee, from John Lewis), IKEA TRADFRI (£8-£25, Zigbee, from IKEA), WiZ (£10-£20, WiFi, from B&Q), TP-Link Kasa (£10-£15, WiFi, from Amazon). Smart switches require a neutral wire - many UK homes pre-2004 lack this. Sonoff, Lightwave, and BG Electrical offer UK-compatible switches. Colour temperature adjustable bulbs (2200K-6500K) allow warm evenings and cool daylight.

---

## Room-by-Room Smart Lighting Setup

Living Room: Smart bulbs in ceiling and lamps. Scenes: Movie (dimmed warm), Reading (bright cool), Relax (warm low). Hallway: Motion-activated bulb - ideal for UK homes where switches are at one end. Kitchen: Under-cabinet LED strips (£20-£50 from Philips Hue or IKEA). Bedroom: Wake-up routine - gradually brighten over 30 minutes before alarm. External: Weatherproof smart lights (IP44+, Philips Hue £50-£90, Ring Pathlights £60-£100). Connect to dusk-to-dawn automation. All outdoor lights must be IP44 or higher for UK weather.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>Can I install smart bulbs in UK B22 fittings?</dt>
<dd>Yes - most brands offer B22 versions. Philips Hue, WiZ, IKEA TRADFRI, and TP-Link Kasa all sell B22 smart bulbs.</dd>

<dt>Do I need a neutral wire for smart switches?</dt>
<dd>Yes - most UK smart switches require neutral. Homes pre-2004 often lack neutral at switches.</dd>

<dt>Can smart lighting reduce my electricity bill?</dt>
<dd>Yes - LED smart bulbs use 80-90% less than halogens. Estimated savings: £30-£80/year for a typical UK 3-bed home. For more information see our <a href="../smart-home/01-smart-home-hub-setup-guide-uk.md">Smart Home Hub Setup Guide UK</a>.</dd>
