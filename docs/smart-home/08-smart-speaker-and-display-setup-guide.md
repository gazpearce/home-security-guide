# Smart Speaker and Display Setup Guide

Guide to setting up smart speakers and displays for UK homes covering Amazon Echo, Google Nest Hub, Apple HomePod, multi-room audio, voice assistant configuration, and privacy settings.

---

## Smart Speaker Selection for UK Homes

Amazon Echo (45% market share): Echo Dot £45-£55 (budget), Echo Studio £180-£220 (premium audio, Zigbee hub), Echo Show 8 £110-£130 (smart display). Google Nest: Nest Audio £80-£100, Nest Hub £80-£100 (display, sleep sensing), Nest Hub Max £190-£220 (with camera). Apple: HomePod mini £99 (Thread border router). Audio: Echo Studio > HomePod mini > Nest Audio > Echo Dot. Multi-room: Amazon Multi-Room Music, Google Speaker Groups, Apple AirPlay 2.

---

## Setup and Configuration

Step 1: Position at ear height on stable surface away from walls. Step 2: Download companion app. Step 3: Connect to WiFi, sign in. Step 4: Set language to English (United Kingdom) for UK spellings and Celsius. Step 5: Configure privacy - review voice history, physical mute button. Step 6: Set up voice profiles for personalised responses. Step 7: Test weather, news, timers, music, smart home control. Step 8: For smart displays, set photo frame to show albums. Step 9: Configure do not disturb (10pm-7am). Step 10: For multi-room, group speakers in the app.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>Which has the best sound quality?</dt>
<dd>Echo Studio with Dolby Atmos. For small spaces, HomePod mini has impressive bass. Nest Audio has balanced sound.</dd>

<dt>Can I use multiple speakers in different rooms?</dt>
<dd>Yes - all three platforms support multi-room audio for synchronised music.</dd>

<dt>Do smart speakers listen to everything?</dt>
<dd>They listen only for the wake word. Audio is processed locally. Review and delete voice history in the app. For more information see our <a href="../smart-home/07-smart-doorbell-installation-guide-uk.md">Smart Doorbell Installation Guide UK</a>.</dd>

</dl>
