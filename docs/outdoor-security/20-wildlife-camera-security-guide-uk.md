# Wildlife Camera Security Guide UK

Guide to using wildlife trail cameras for garden security in the UK including placement, battery life, night vision, and image storage.

---

## Trail Cameras for Garden Security

Trail cameras offer covert monitoring at a fraction of CCTV costs (£40 - £200 vs £150 - £600). Brands include Browning, Bushnell, and Reolink  -  the Browning Recon Force 4K (£180) offers 4K video and 30-metre night vision. Typical installation: mount on a fence post at 1.5 metres, angled down at 30 degrees. Most cameras use 8x AA batteries lasting 3 - 6 months. PIR detection range is 20 - 30 metres with a 0.3 - 0.5 second trigger speed. Trail cameras are portable and can be rotated between vulnerable areas.

---

## Data Storage and Legal Use in the UK

Trail cameras store images on microSD cards up to 512GB  -  a 64GB card holds 50,000+ images. Battery-powered units need no external wiring, suitable for remote allotments and garden boundaries. Under UK law, trail cameras on your own property are legal as long as they do not capture neighbours' houses or public spaces. The ICO recommends clear signage even for covert cameras. For long-term monitoring, wire a 12V external battery pack (£25 - £50) to avoid winter battery drain. Lithium AA batteries perform better in UK winter conditions.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>Are trail cameras legal for home security in the UK?</dt>
<dd>Yes, on your own property and not aimed at neighbours or public spaces. Signage is recommended by the ICO.</dd>

<dt>What battery type works best for UK winter use?</dt>
<dd>Lithium AA batteries maintain performance below 0 degreesC. Alkaline cells lose 50%+ capacity in sub-zero UK winter temperatures.</dd>

<dt>Do trail cameras send alerts to my phone?</dt>
<dd>Some models with 4G LTE or Wi-Fi connectivity offer real-time alerts  -  check for 'cellular' or 'smart' in the product name. For more information see our <a href="11-hedge-and-planting-security-uk.md">Hedge and Planting Security UK</a>.</dd>
