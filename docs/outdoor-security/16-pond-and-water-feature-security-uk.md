# Pond and Water Feature Security UK

Guide to securing garden ponds, water features, pumps, and equipment from theft and vandalism in UK gardens.

---

## Pond Equipment Theft Risks

ONS data on garden theft indicates pond pumps, filters, and decorative items worth £2,000 - £5,000 are common targets. Pond pumps from Oase, Hozelock, and Blagdon cost £50 - £400. Pond vacuums (£150 - £350) and UV clarifiers (£60 - £200) are also frequently stolen. Secure equipment by housing pumps inside a lockable external box (Screwfix, £30 - £80) or a brick-built chamber with a padlocked lid. Use stainless steel M8 security bolts (£5 - £12) to anchor filters to concrete bases.

---

## Securing Koi and Ornamental Fish

Koi carp valued at hundreds or thousands of pounds are targeted by organised theft. Install a pond net cover (from £30 - £100 per m2), motion-activated lighting around the pond, and a submersible alarm sensor that triggers on water disturbance. Koi thefts rose 22% in 2024 according to the Koi Water Garden Association. Use a wildlife camera overlooking the pond with night vision  -  the Reolink Argus 3 Pro (£110) is popular for pond monitoring.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>How do I stop my pond pump being stolen?</dt>
<dd>House the pump in a lockable ABS box bolted to concrete. Use stainless steel security bolts with tamper-proof heads.</dd>

<dt>Are pond fish often stolen in the UK?</dt>
<dd>Yes  -  koi carp theft has increased significantly. High-value collections are targeted, with losses of £1,000 - £10,000 reported.</dd>

<dt>Should I insure my pond equipment?</dt>
<dd>Yes  -  specialist garden insurance or a rider on your home insurance covers pond equipment. Check the policy value limit. For more information see our <a href="15-garden-office-security-guide-uk.md">Garden Office Security Guide UK</a>.</dd>
