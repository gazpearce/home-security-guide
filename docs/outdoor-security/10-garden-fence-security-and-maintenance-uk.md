# Garden Fence Security and Maintenance UK

Guide to reinforcing and maintaining garden fences for security, privacy, and boundary protection for UK homes.

---

## Fence Security Weaknesses

ONS data shows 27% of UK burglars enter a property by climbing or breaching a fence. Standard 1.8m fence panels can be pushed aside easily  -  install gravel boards and concrete posts to anchor them. Prices for fence upgrades: concrete gravel boards £12 - £25 each from B&Q, posts £8 - £18, closeboard panels £30 - £60. Use security spikes or trellis topping (Screwfix, £10 - £30 per 2m section) to deter climbers. Paladin and Jacksons Fencing offer security-rated systems from £80 - £150 per metre.

---

## Maintenance for Long-Term Security

Rotting fence panels lose 70% of their strength  -  treat timber fence posts annually with wood preservative (£15 - £25 from Ronseal or Cuprinol). Replace any loose or missing fence boards immediately; gaps create footholds. Metal fences from Grange Fencing (£40 - £80 per 2m panel) offer superior durability over timber. For boundary hedges, maintain a dense 1.8 - 2.5m height. Install a gate alarm if the fence encloses a full perimeter.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>What is the most secure garden fencing?</dt>
<dd>Concrete closeboard or metal fencing with anti-climb tops offers the best security. Timber panels are the least secure.</dd>

<dt>Can I put anti-climb paint on my fence legally?</dt>
<dd>Yes, but you must display a warning sign. Anti-climb paint is a deterrent, not a harmful substance.</dd>

<dt>How high can a garden fence be without planning permission?</dt>
<dd>2 metres maximum without planning permission. Fences over 1 metre adjacent to a highway also need permission. For more information see our <a href="05-garden-gate-security-and-locks-uk.md">Garden Gate Security and Locks UK</a>.</dd>
</dl>
