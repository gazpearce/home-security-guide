# Shed Security Devices and Alarms UK

Comprehensive guide to shed security alarms, locking systems, and anti-theft devices for UK garden sheds and outbuildings.

---

## Shed Theft Statistics and Risk in the UK

ONS data shows over 350,000 shed and outbuilding thefts occur annually in the UK, with average losses of £1,200 per incident. Shed alarms from Yale, Ring, and Screwfix cost £10 - £80. A standalone alarm with a 100dB siren (£25 - £50) is a strong deterrent  -  68% of shed burglars are deterred by audible alarms. Padlocks alone are insufficient; 72% of shed break-ins involve cutting a padlock. Use a closed-shackle design and a hasp with concealed screws.

---

## Locking Systems and Security Hardware

The best shed security uses multiple layers: a 5-lever mortice deadlock on the door, two heavy-duty padlocks with different key profiles, and hinge bolts on the door side. Secure shed windows with grilles (Screwfix, £15 - £40) or security film. Anchor the shed to a concrete base using expanding resin bolts. For sheds containing bikes or high-value tools, add a battery-powered PIR alarm with interior coverage. Check shed insurance requirements  -  many UK policies mandate specific locks for cover.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>Do shed alarms really deter burglars?</dt>
<dd>Yes  -  68% of shed burglars are deterred by a 100dB+ siren. Combined with a visible alarm box, the deterrent effect increases significantly.</dd>

<dt>What insurance requirements apply to shed security?</dt>
<dd>Most UK insurers require a 5-lever mortice deadlock (BS3621) and a closed-shackle padlock for contents over £1,000. Check your policy wording.</dd>

<dt>Can I connect a shed alarm to my home system?</dt>
<dd>Yes  -  smart alarms like Ring and Yale connect via Z-Wave or Wi-Fi. Ensure the shed is within 30 metres of your home router. For more information see our <a href="07-garage-security-guide-uk.md">Garage Security Guide UK</a>.</dd>
</dl>
