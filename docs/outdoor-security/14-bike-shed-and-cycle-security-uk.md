# Bike Shed and Cycle Security UK

Guide to bike shed and cycle storage security for UK homes including locks, alarms, and insurance requirements for bicycles.

---

## Bike Theft Statistics in the UK

ONS data shows over 380,000 bicycles are stolen in the UK annually  -  one every 90 seconds. The average bike value is £750, with e-bikes averaging £2,000+. A third of bike thefts occur from sheds and garages. Dedicated bike storage: metal bike sheds (£150 - £500 from Asgard), heavy-duty ground anchors (£20 - £80 from Screwfix), and wall-mounted racks (£15 - £50 from Halfords). Insurance-compliant security requires a Sold Secure GOLD or Diamond-rated lock  -  brands include Kryptonite, Abus, and Squire (£40 - £120).

---

## Bike Storage and Anchoring Methods

For shed storage, install a ground anchor bolted through the shed floor into a concrete base using M12 expanding bolts. The Asgard Access 3 bike store (£399) has 1mm steel construction and a 5-lever locking system. Inside the shed, use the Squire ground anchor (£32) and a Kryptonite New York Fahgettaboudit chain lock (£95, 14mm hardened steel). Remove the front wheel when storing to make the bike less easily ridden away. Register bikes at BikeRegister.com  -  marked bikes are 60% less likely to be stolen.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>What lock rating do I need for bike insurance?</dt>
<dd>Most UK insurers require a Sold Secure GOLD or Diamond-rated lock. Check your policy  -  some demand both a D-lock and a chain.</dd>

<dt>Are metal bike sheds more secure than wooden?</dt>
<dd>Yes  -  metal sheds from Asgard and Biohort with integral locks are much harder to break into than wooden sheds with retrofitted locks.</dd>

<dt>Where should I store my bike to prevent theft?</dt>
<dd>Inside your home is safest. If unavailable, use a metal bike shed with ground anchor and two Sold Secure GOLD+ locks. For more information see our <a href="06-shed-security-devices-and-alarms-uk.md">Shed Security Devices and Alarms UK</a>.</dd>
</dl>
