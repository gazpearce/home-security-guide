## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>Can smart locks help elderly people stay independent?</dt>
<dd>Yes - eliminating key operation reduces a significant daily challenge. Voice control, fingerprint, and key fob options provide accessible alternatives.</dd>

<dt>Is there funding available for smart locks for disabled users?</dt>
<dd>Disabled Facilities Grants (DFG) up to £30,000 may cover smart home adaptations. Contact your local UK social services for an OT assessment.</dd>

<dt>Can I monitor an elderly relative's door activity remotely?</dt>
<dd>Yes - share the activity log with family members. Set notifications for unusual patterns (no entry by 10am, door used at 3am). For more information see our <a href="../smart-locks/35-smart-lock-children-safety-features-guide.md">Smart Lock Children Safety Features Guide</a>.</dd>

</dl>
