# Smart Lock Auto-Lock and Geofencing Guide

Guide to auto-lock and geofencing features on UK smart locks covering automatic locking configuration, proximity-based unlock, safety considerations, and battery impact.

---

## Auto-Lock Features and Configuration

Auto-lock automatically locks your door after it has been opened and closed, preventing accidental unlocked doors. Recommended UK setting: 30 seconds - long enough to exit but short enough to prevent the door remaining unlocked. Instant lock (0 seconds) can trap users stepping out briefly. Auto-lock uses a door position sensor (magnetic contact or accelerometer). Ensure correct alignment - misalignment is the most common cause of failure. Some locks support conditional auto-lock (only overnight or when no one is home via geofencing). Auto-lock draws slightly more battery power (5-10% reduction).

---

## Geofencing Configuration

Geofencing uses phone GPS to trigger lock actions. Set radius to 100-200 metres for typical UK homes. Arrival: auto-unlock when you approach. The lock detects your phone via Bluetooth within 20-30 metres. Departure: auto-lock when all family members have left. Safety: auto-unlock should require the phone on your person. Configure PIN backup for dead phone batteries. Multi-phone households: the lock should only auto-unlock when the primary phone approaches. Most apps allow per-user geofencing settings. Battery impact: geofencing uses phone GPS (5-10% daily phone drain). Lock Bluetooth polling increases consumption by 10-15%.

## Specifications and Comparison

| Feature | Recommendation | UK Consideration | Battery Impact |
| ---|---|---|--- |
| Auto-Lock Delay | 30 seconds | Standard for UK homes | 5-10% reduction |
| Auto-Unlock Radius | 100-200m | UK terraced house separation | Minimal |
| Geofencing Type | Bluetooth + GPS hybrid | Works in UK urban areas | 10-15% reduction |
| Door Sensor | Magnetic contact | Check alignment monthly | No battery impact |
| Manual Override | Always available | Essential for UK winter | No impact |

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>Can auto-lock be dangerous?</dt>
<dd>Set the delay to 30 seconds minimum. Most locks include a privacy mode that temporarily disables auto-lock.</dd>

<dt>Does geofencing work in UK flats?</dt>
<dd>Yes - GPS works at building level. Bluetooth range in flats with concrete floors may be reduced.</dd>

<dt>Will auto-unlock work if my phone is in my bag?</dt>
<dd>Yes - Bluetooth proximity detection works through bags and pockets within 5-10 metres. For more information see our <a href="../smart-locks/12-smart-lock-temporary-access-codes-guide.md">Smart Lock Temporary Access Codes Guide</a>.</dd>

</dl>
