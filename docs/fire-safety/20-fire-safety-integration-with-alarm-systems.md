# Fire Safety Integration with Alarm Systems

Integrating fire safety with home security and smart alarm systems in UK homes  -  hybrid detection, central monitoring and home insurance.

---

## Combining Fire and Intruder Alarm Systems

Integrating fire detection with your intruder alarm system provides comprehensive property protection and can reduce insurance premiums by 5-15% with UK insurers. Modern hybrid alarm panels from Texecom, Pyronix, and Honeywell support both intruder zones and fire detection zones (smoke, heat, CO detectors wired into the same panel). A combined system costs £400-£1,000 for installation depending on property size. Key benefits include: central monitoring via an ARC (Alarm Receiving Centre) that dispatches fire services automatically, remote arming/disarming via smartphone, and unified keypad control. BS 8243-compliant alarm monitoring adds £15-£30 per month. Fire detectors on an intruder system must be interconnected with standalone smoke alarms to ensure the fire alarm is audible throughout the property.

---

## Smart Home Fire Safety Ecosystems

Smart home platforms like Samsung SmartThings, Hubitat, and Homey allow fire alarms, CO detectors, and smart smoke alarms to trigger automations  -  such as turning on lights when an alarm sounds, unlocking doors for escape, and sending smartphone notifications. The Aico HomeLINK system provides a gateway hub (£60) that connects up to 50 alarms to the cloud, with landlord remote monitoring and real-time push alerts. A fully integrated smart fire safety system for a 3-bed home costs £300-£600 including smart alarms, hub, and automation setup. Ensure all smart fire devices are certified to BS EN 14604 or BS 50291. Voice alarms that announce the danger type and location are available from Nest Protect and FireAngel Connected, providing clearer instruction during evacuation.
