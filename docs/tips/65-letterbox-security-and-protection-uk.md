# Letterbox Security and Protection UK

Stop thieves fishing keys, documents, and packages through your letterbox with these proven measures.

---

## Letterbox Fishing  -  How It Works and How to Stop It

Letterbox fishing  -  using a hooked wire or tape through the flap to grab keys  -  accounts for 8 % of all burglaries in England and Wales, according to ONS data. The simplest fix is a letterbox cage (Yale, from £14.99) that fits on the interior side and prevents anything being hooked. A brush-type seal (Sto , £8.99) restricts access while reducing drafts. If you have a cat flap next to the letterbox, relocate or secure it  -  thieves use both openings together. Never leave keys on a hallway table within arm's reach of the flap.

---

## Reinforcing Your Letterbox Against Forced Entry

Vulnerable flaps can be forced open with a crowbar. Upgrade to a steel security letterbox plate (ERA, £24.99) that bolts through the door and resists prying. The Letterbox Buddy (£34.99) is a British-designed internal guard that locks with a key when you are out. For parcels, a parcel drop box (Parceluxe, £149.99) allows secure delivery. Replace single-glazed transom panels above the door with reinforced glass or polycarbonate (8 mm sheet from £25). Secured by Design recommends that letterbox openings never exceed 260 mm x 50 mm.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>What is letterbox fishing?</dt>
<dd>A thief uses a hooked wire through the flap to grab keys or valuables placed nearby.</dd>

<dt>Do brush seals stop fishing?</dt>
<dd>Partially  -  they resist hooks, but a cage is the only 100 % effective solution.</dd>

<dt>Can I put my letterbox on the wall instead?</dt>
