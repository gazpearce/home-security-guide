# Smart Intercom System Installation UK

Guide to installing smart video intercom systems in UK homes and flats, including wired digital systems, WiFi intercoms, and integration with existing entry phones.

---

## Types of Smart Intercom Systems for UK Properties

Smart intercom systems replace traditional hardwired entry phones with app-controlled video door entry, covering everything from ground-floor flats to multi-storey conversions. The UK market breaks into three categories: WiFi intercoms (Ring Intercom, £199.00), PoE video intercoms (Hikvision DS-KD8003, £289.00), and retrofit modules like the Aiphone AI-801 (£219.00) that add smartphone control to existing 2-wire entry phones. According to IHS Markit, UK smart intercom sales reached 890,000 units in 2025, driven by flats and HMOs. WiFi intercoms are easiest to install  -  the Ring Intercom fits over your existing entry phone handset with no wiring. PoE intercoms require a CAT6 cable run and a PoE+ switch (802.3at, 30W per port) but offer superior video quality (4K vs 1080p) and integration with access control systems. For multi-tenant buildings, a centralised video intercom system with a PRAM (Power Riser Amplifier Module) distributes video and audio to all flats from a single outdoor panel. These systems must comply with BS 5839-6 (fire alarm) and the Equality Act 2010  -  tactile buttons and induction loop support are recommended.

---

## Wiring a Smart Intercom with Existing UK Entry Phone Cables

Older UK flats (pre-2000) typically use 2-wire analogue entry phones from brands like Fermax, Aiphone, or BPT. These use a simple analogue audio-only circuit with no video. To upgrade to a smart video intercom, you have three options. First, the retrofit module approach: devices like the Aiphone AI-801 wire into the existing 2-wire bus, adding a camera and WiFi module without replacing the outdoor panel. Second, full replacement: pull the outdoor panel and replace with a Hikvision DS-KD8003 PoE panel, running a CAT6 cable from the flat's existing entry phone location. Third, wireless replacement: replace both indoor and outdoor units with a WiFi system like the Ring Intercom, which requires only the existing 230V mains supply at the indoor handset location. For all options, the fire safety requirement under BS 5839-6 mandates that residents can override the electronic lock release manually from inside the flat, and that the door release fails unlocked on power loss. Installation costs range from £150 (retrofit module, DIY) to £800 (full PoE replacement with electrician).

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>Can I use a Ring Intercom in a UK flat with existing entry phone wiring?</dt>
<dd>Yes. The Ring Intercom (£199.00) retrofits over most UK 2-wire entry phone handsets without rewiring. It connects to the existing handset via a bracket and runs on 230V mains supply already at the indoor unit.</dd>

<dt>Do smart intercoms need to comply with UK fire safety regulations?</dt>
<dd>Yes. BS 5839-6 requires that residents can manually override the electronic lock release from inside the flat and that the door fails unlocked on power loss. Ensure your installer certifies compliance.</dd>

<dt>What wiring is needed for a PoE video intercom system?</dt>
<dd>CAT6 cable from the outdoor panel to a PoE+ switch (802.3at, 30W). The outdoor panel requires a minimum of 15W for the camera and audio. The cable run must not exceed 100 metres from the switch. For more information see our <a href="07-apartment-entry-phone.md">Apartment Entry Phone Upgrade UK</a>.</dd>
</dl>
