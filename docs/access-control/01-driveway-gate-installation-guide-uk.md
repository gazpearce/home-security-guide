# Driveway Gate Installation Guide UK

Complete guide to installing driveway gates on UK properties, covering planning permission, gate types, and professional installation costs.

---

## Choosing the Right Driveway Gate Type

Selecting the right driveway gate depends on your property layout, budget, and access requirements. In the UK, the most common types are swing gates and sliding gates. Swing gates are ideal for properties with ample driveway depth and typically cost between £1,200 and £3,500 for a pair of 3m galvanised steel leaves from brands like BFT, FAAC, or DoorHan. Sliding gates suit sloping or short driveways and range from £2,000 to £5,500 installed. Timber gates offer a traditional aesthetic but require yearly staining and can warp over time, while aluminium composite gates are low-maintenance and corrosion-resistant, popular in coastal areas. Automated gates add £800 - £2,000 for a 230V operator kit. Always verify your property boundaries before ordering  -  disputes over shared driveways are one of the most common causes of installation delays in the UK.

---

## Planning Permission and Legal Requirements

Most residential driveway gates under 2m height do not require planning permission in England and Wales, provided they are set back from the highway by at least 1m. Gates opening outward onto a public road or footpath are restricted under the Highway Act 1980 and require a Section 184 licence from your local highways authority. Listed buildings or properties in conservation areas face stricter rules  -  you may need listed building consent or article 4 direction approval. Gates must not obstruct the public highway or create a visibility hazard. Many UK councils also require that automated gates comply with BS EN 13241-1 and the Supply of Machinery (Safety) Regulations 2008. A structural survey of your gate pillars is recommended if you plan to install heavy iron or timber gates, as inadequate foundations are a leading cause of gate failure within five years.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>Do I need planning permission for driveway gates in the UK?</dt>
<dd>In most cases, no  -  provided the gate is under 2m tall and set back at least 1m from the highway. Gates that open outward onto a public road require a Section 184 licence under the Highway Act 1980.</dd>

<dt>How much does driveway gate installation cost in the UK?</dt>
<dd>A basic pair of manual swing gates starts at around £1,200 installed. Automated systems with a gate opener, safety sensors, and electrical work typically range from £2,500 to £6,000 depending on gate material and automation complexity.</dd>

<dt>What is the best material for UK driveway gates?</dt>
<dd>Galvanised steel with powder coating offers the best balance of strength, security, and weather resistance. Aluminium composite is preferred in coastal areas, while timber suits traditional settings but demands regular maintenance.
