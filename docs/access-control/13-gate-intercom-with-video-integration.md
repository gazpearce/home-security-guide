# Gate Intercom with Video Integration

UK guide to video intercom systems for gates, covering HD cameras, night vision, smartphone app integration, and wiring best practices for 2025 installations.

---

## Video Intercom Features and Camera Technology

Video intercom systems combine a gate entry intercom with a high-definition camera, allowing residents to see and speak to visitors before granting access. Modern UK video intercoms feature 1080p or 2K resolution cameras with wide-angle lenses (100 degrees - 160 degrees diagonal field of view), infrared LED arrays for night vision up to 10 metres, and wide dynamic range (WDR) to handle backlit conditions common in UK winter light. Leading systems include BFT Avel, Came BXS Touch, DoorHan Ultra SC, and Fermax Cityplus. Prices range from £300 for a basic 2-wire colour video kit with indoor monitor to £1,500 for a premium IP-based system with app control, cloud recording, and motion detection alerts. Units with a built-in microSD card slot for local recording cost an extra £20 - £50. Two-way audio with noise cancellation is essential for UK wind-prone locations  -  look for systems with DSP echo cancellation and a wind reduction microphone cover.

---

## Installation, Networking and Smart Home Integration

Installing a video intercom at a UK gate requires a 4-core screened cable for analogue systems (up to 100 m) or a Cat6a shielded cable for IP/PoE systems (up to 100 m for PoE, extendable with a switch). The camera should be mounted at eye level (1.5 - 1.6 m) for optimal facial capture, angled down slightly to avoid glare from direct sunlight. Networked systems require Power over Ethernet (PoE) from a PoE switch or injector, or a local 12V DC supply. For existing gates without cabling, wireless video intercoms with a dedicated Wi-Fi link are available at £400 - £900, though signal reliability depends on distance and building materials. Smart home integration via Amazon Alexa or Google Home allows announcements when someone rings  -  the video feed appears on compatible smart displays. Cloud recording subscriptions cost £3 - £8 per month per unit. GDPR requires that recorded visitor footage be stored securely, with automatic overwrite after 30 days for non-incident footage.

## FAQ

<dl>

<dt>What camera resolution do I need for a UK gate video intercom?</dt>
<dd>1080p (2 MP) is the minimum recommended for clear facial identification. 2K (4 MP) offers better detail for wider gate openings. Infrared night vision to at least 5 m is essential for UK winter evenings.</dd>

<dt>Can I view my gate video intercom on my phone?</dt>
<dd>Yes. IP-based systems like BFT Avel, Came Connect, and 2N Mobile Key support live video streaming and two-way talk on iOS and Android apps. Remote unlock is also available from anywhere.</dd>

<dt>Do I need Wi-Fi at the gate for a video intercom?</dt>
<dd>For IP systems, yes. Alternatively, 4G intercoms work without Wi-Fi by using a SIM card in the outdoor station. Analogue 2-wire systems connect directly to the indoor monitor without internet. For more information see our <a href="06-intercom-gate-entry-system-uk.md">Intercom Gate Entry System UK</a>.</dd>
</dl>
